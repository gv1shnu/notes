The structure: The Method

10. Managers & engines.

A use case is a sequence of activities. There are only two things that can change — the sequence or the activities — and each gets its own kind of component.

Manager Engine
Encapsulates the sequence (a family of related use cases) an activity / business rule
Named like a noun (BankManager) a verb (CalculationEngine)
Wrong name BillingManager (verb → functional!) AccountEngine (noun → functional!)

Engines are rare and shared across managers. There's a rough golden ratio (Fibonacci-ish): 1 manager → 0–1 engines, 2 → 1, 3 → 2. Eight managers means you've already failed. An explosion of engines is functional decomposition hiding — if there are only 2–3 ways to do something, that's a switch statement, not a component.

Watch the trap: volatile ≠ variable. Variability is just code; a component earns its place only for genuine open-ended volatility.
