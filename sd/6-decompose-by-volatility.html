The cure: volatility-based decomposition

Difficulty: Medium

6. Decompose by volatility.

Four words are the whole of software architecture: decompose based on volatility. Find the areas of change, encapsulate each in a building block, and implement behavior as interactions between them.

Think of each block as a vault. A change is a hand grenade: open the right vault, toss it in, shut the door. Inside may be destroyed — but no shrapnel flies through the rest of the system. The change is contained.

Drop the change into the one vault built to absorb it. The blast wrecks the inside, but the thick walls stop the shrapnel — everything outside carries on unaffected. Containing change, not preventing it, is the whole game.

Volatility Encapsulated behind
AC/DC, 110/220V, grid/solar/battery the wall receptacle — you just plug in
blood pressure, pulse, adrenaline the heart
every difference between two laptops the HDMI plug

Contrast functional decomposition: the change isn't in any one place, so (Murphy) it's in all places. It maximizes the impact of change — the real nail in the coffin. Volatility doesn't announce itself, though; customers talk features, not change. You have to sweat to find it (which is exactly why it can add value).
