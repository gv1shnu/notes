The structure: The Method

12. Required behaviour, not functionality.

Capture how the system must behave, not what it must do. "System should open a file" is functional and open to interpretation; the behavioural version pins it down:

Functional Behavioural
open a file show the open dialog, filtered to *.x; only let the user reach paths they have permission for; hold a write-exclusive lock; auto-release after 3 idle hours and log it

A sequence of required behaviours is a use case (use case, not user case — the consumer may be another system; like Google, most of a system is below the waterline). Managers encapsulate the sequencing of use cases; engines, the activities within them.
