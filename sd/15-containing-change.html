Difficulty: Hard

15. Containing change = agility.

You can't stop change; the trick is minimizing its impact. Functional decomposition maximizes it. The Method contains it — a change in required behaviour hits the workflow, which lives in one manager.

On a behaviour change…
the manager may be rewritten — that's fine, it's cheap
engines, resource-access, resources, clients, utilities untouched — and that's where the bulk of the effort lives

The expensive work (engines, atomic-verb resource access, scalable resources, real UX, good APIs, infrastructure) is salvaged — change can't reach it. That ability to respond quickly to changing behaviour is agility.

Most teams "do agile" — the rituals and artifacts — without being agile. You can only be agile by containing change, and you only contain change with volatility-based decomposition.
