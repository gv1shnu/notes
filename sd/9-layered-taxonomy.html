The structure: The Method

Difficulty: Medium

Software isn't a house or a jet — the volatile areas repeat across systems. So there's a reusable template: four layers, each encapsulating what's below it, plus utilities to the side.

9. The layered taxonomy.

Layer Encapsulates Question
Clients how the system is consumed (UI, mobile, another system) who
Managers volatility in the sequence of a use case what
Engines volatility in the activities / business rules how
ResourceAccess how resources are accessed how (access)
Resources the actual stores (DB, queue, other systems) where

Utilities (security, logging, pub/sub, message bus, diagnostics) sit in a vertical bar to the side, usable by every layer. "Client" beats "presentation" — a consumer may be another system, nothing is presented. Equalising all clients gives you a shot at a single point of entry.

Each layer answers one question — Clients (who), Managers (what), Engines (how), ResourceAccess (how, access), Resources (where) — and calls only the layer directly below. Utilities run alongside, reachable by all. The same skeleton fits almost any system.

It's abstract on purpose: a mouse and an elephant have the same architecture; a mouse and a grasshopper do not.
