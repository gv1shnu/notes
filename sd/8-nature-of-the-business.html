The cure: volatility-based decomposition

8. Don't encapsulate the business.

New converts over-encapsulate — a box for every "this could change". The guardrail: never encapsulate changes to the nature of the business. Those are vanishingly rare, and when you try, you do a poor job.

A single-family home could become a 30-floor skyscraper — but ~1 in 10 million per year, and your friction-pile foundations and private transformer would be a botch. That's not design, it's speculative design (think high heels with built-in scuba flippers: bad at both).

The competitor test. To find real volatility, design for your arch-nemesis. Wave a magic wand and lift FedEx's code onto UPS's servers, repaint the trucks — will it run?

FedEx vs UPS Verdict
insure via Lloyd's vs self-insure differs → insurance is volatile, encapsulate it
both ship packages, both track same → nature of the business, do NOT encapsulate

Where you and your competitor do something the same way, it's the nature of the business — leave it.
