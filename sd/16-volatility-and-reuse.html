Validating the design

Difficulty: Hard

A few attributes tell you a design is on the right track — or not.

16. The volatility & reuse gradient.

In a good design, volatility decreases top-down and reuse increases top-down:

Layer Volatility Reuse
Clients high (churn: mobile, web, blue, vanilla) none
Managers medium some
Engines low more
ResourceAccess lower high
Resources tiny (sentinels) reused across systems

This must hold: lower layers are depended on the most, so if they were also the most volatile, the system would detonate from below. Also, managers should be "almost expendable" — like this laptop: losing one costs money, but plug in another and nothing else in your life changes. If a manager change makes you shrug ("whatever") it's a pass-through in name only; if it makes you panic ("oh no") it's too coupled. The right reaction is "hmm, I'd go into that area."

No gradient, no expendable managers → you still have functional decomposition hiding. Keep digging.
