The disease: functional decomposition

2. Why it fails.

Functional decomposition is also time decomposition: B means "after A, before C". Lift B out to reuse it and A dangles off the front, C off the back. So the problems compound:

Problem Why
No reuse a block is glued to the steps before and after it
Duplication so everyone reinvents the wheel across systems
Bloated client someone must sequence A→B→C; that someone is the client → business logic leaks into it
Many entry points enter for A, for B, for C → security/scaling nightmares (good design wants one entry)
Explosion or god-service 300 tiny blocks (nonlinear integration cost) or one monolith (diabolical internal complexity)

Try to fix it by making A call B call C instead, and it gets worse: A must know about B, B about C, and all the failure/undo logic of A and B leaks into C. Three "services" become one giant clump of doom — and the complexity grows non-linearly.
