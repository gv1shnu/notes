The disease: functional decomposition

Difficulty: Easy

Architecture is always an act of decomposition — you take one big idea and break it into building blocks. The only question is: decompose by what?

1. Functional decomposition.

Decomposing by the required functionality: a requirement to do A, B, C becomes an A block, a B block, a C block.

Requirement Block
do billing Billing
do shipping Shipping
do invoicing Invoicing

It's simple, it's easy, and almost everyone does it. That doesn't make it right. Löwy's claim: "If that is your architecture, the project has already failed — in past tense, before anyone wrote the first line of code."

A single business change — adding a tax rule — has to be threaded through Billing and Shipping and Invoicing, because each "does" a function that the change cuts across. Decompose by function and change lands everywhere at once. That is the failure, baked in before any code.
