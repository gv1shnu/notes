The disease: functional decomposition

5. Solutions masquerading as requirements.

The customer says cooking is the #1 requirement — bold, underlined, 1.0.0. But is cooking really a requirement? A year later they ask to order pizza. There's no box for pizza.

Stated "requirement" Really the requirement?
Cooking no — a solution
Feeding closer (covers pizza, dining out) — but diet day breaks it
Well-being the real requirement (food, temperature, comfort)

Almost every spec and sticky-note board is solutions masquerading as requirements. Design against them and you chase an endless parade of "features" forever. The fix is to look past each stated solution to the underlying thing that can change — which is the subject of the cure.
