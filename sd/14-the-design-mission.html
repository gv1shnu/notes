Composing the system

14. The design mission.

The bar: your architecture must satisfy all use cases — present and future, known and unknown. If a future use case can invalidate your architecture, you failed. Nothing less will do.

That sounds impossible — you can't know the future. But you don't design against the use cases; you design the small set of blocks that can be composed into any of them. A spec of 300 use cases is untrustworthy anyway:

A "complete" 300-use-case spec is…
missing some (nobody ever specs them all)
full of duplicates (different teams, different words)
contradictory (cooking AND dieting), and full of solutions-as-requirements

So the one rule that eludes whole careers: never design against the requirements. Garbage in, garbage out — you've always known the requirements are garbage, yet treated them as gospel. And requirements will change — which is good: change is the hand that feeds us. Don't fight it.
