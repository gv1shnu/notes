Grover's Search

Now an algorithm with real-world teeth. The problem: an unstructured list of N items — no sorting, no order — and you want the one that matches. Think finding a name in a shuffled phone book by value, not position.

The speed-up

Approach Lookups
Classical (check one by one) O(N) — on average N/2
Grover O(√N)

Not the exponential leap of Shor, but a quadratic one that applies to a huge range of "search / satisfy this condition" problems — a billion items in ~31,000 steps instead of a billion.

How it works: amplitude amplification

Start with every item in equal superposition, then repeat two moves:

Move Effect
Oracle flips the phase of the winning item (marks it)
Diffusion "reflect about the average" — nudges every amplitude toward the mean

The oracle pushes the winner's amplitude just below the average; the diffusion step reflects everything across that average, which yanks the winner up and pushes the rest down. Each pair of moves makes the winner a little more likely. Do it ~√N times and the winner dominates the measurement.

The catch worth knowing: more isn't better. Overshoot the ~√N iterations and the amplitude sails past certainty and starts shrinking again — the probability oscillates. For 4 items, exactly one iteration nails it with certainty; knowing when to stop is part of the algorithm.
