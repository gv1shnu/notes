Bra-ket Notation

Writing out full column matrices for every qubit gets old fast — and we've only seen one qubit. Dirac's bra-ket notation (yes, as in "bracket", and no, you don't pronounce the c) is pure shorthand. No new physics, just less ink.

The pieces

Symbol Name Is
|A⟩ ket a column vector (a state)
⟨A| bra its adjoint (†) — the row version
⟨A|E⟩ bra-ket bra × ket = the inner product (a probability)

So ⟨A|E⟩ is exactly "adjoint of A, times E" — the inner product from the qubit page, now one tidy symbol instead of a transpose, a conjugate, and a multiplication spelled out.

The standard kets

Ket Vector
|0⟩ [1; 0]
|1⟩ [0; 1]

The labels 0 and 1 are just names — you don't do arithmetic on them; they point at fixed vectors.

Why it's worth it

Factor the shared 1/√2 out of the superposition state and it collapses to something you can actually read:

[1/√2 ; 1/√2]  =  (1/√2)( |0⟩ + |1⟩ )

That's "an equal blend of 0 and 1" at a glance — and you'll write it roughly a thousand more times. The payoff compounds with more qubits, which (naturally) is the next and final page.
