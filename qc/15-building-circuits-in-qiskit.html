Building Circuits in Qiskit

Difficulty: Hard

We've drawn circuits by hand and pushed states through matrices. Qiskit (IBM's Python SDK) does the bookkeeping so we can build, run, and measure for real. A circuit needs two kinds of register: qubits to compute on, and classical bits to catch the measured results.

"Hello, Quantum" — the Bell state in code

from qiskit import QuantumCircuit
from qiskit_aer import AerSimulator

qc = QuantumCircuit(2, 2)        # 2 qubits, 2 classical bits
qc.h(0)                          # Hadamard on q0 -> superposition
qc.cx(0, 1)                      # CNOT q0->q1   -> entangle
qc.measure([0, 1], [0, 1])       # collapse into the classical bits

sim = AerSimulator()
counts = sim.run(qc, shots=1024).result().get_counts()
print(counts)                   # ~ {'00': 519, '11': 505}

The exact circuit the code builds: H puts q0 into superposition, the CNOT ties q1 to it (control dot on q0, ⊕ target on q1), and the two meters collapse each qubit into a classical bit. Because they are entangled, the meters always agree — only 00 or 11 ever comes out.

Two things that bite beginners

Idea Why it matters
Measurement is mandatory without measure, results never reach the classical bits — you get nothing to read
Shots each run yields one collapsed outcome; running 1024 times reveals the distribution

One shot of the Bell state gives you a single "00" or "11" — useless on its own. Run it 1024 times and the ~50/50 split appears: roughly 512 each, never a 01 or 10. The histogram is the answer. (1024 because a software engineer can't resist a power of two.)

Simulator now, real hardware later

AerSimulator runs the circuit on your laptop — instant, free, perfect for learning. IBM also exposes real quantum computers over the cloud (named after cities), but they come with job queues and genuine noise. The simulator is where you'll live; real hardware is a swap of the backend once the circuit works.

Why it matters: build → measure → run for shots → read the histogram. That loop is every Qiskit program from here on; the algorithms just change what goes between "build" and "measure."
