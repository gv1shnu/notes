Entanglement

The third pillar. To get there we need spin — one of the four numbers that pin down an electron. Despite the name, electrons don't actually spin; "spin" just measures out to one of two values, up or down. Perfect for encoding a bit: down = 0, up = 1.

Spin is a qubit

Before you measure, an electron's spin is in superposition — neither up nor down, but the possibility of both. Measure it and it collapses to one. Measure again and you get the same answer; the first look already settled it. (This is Schrödinger's cat: sealed in a box with a 50/50 poison, it's "alive and dead" until you open the lid — an analogy that has caused more confusion than enlightenment, but here we are.)

The problem: making two qubits agree

When two electrons interact, their spins can become correlated — entangled. Now measuring one instantly tells you the other, with no second measurement needed:

Analogy Open one box… …and you instantly know
Apple / banana in two boxes banana the other is the apple
Entangled spins up the partner is down — instantly

"Instantly" means instantly — faster than light, whether the partner is across the room or across the galaxy. Einstein loathed this and called it "spooky action at a distance", which remains the best name physics has ever produced.

Why it's powerful

The apple/banana boxes are anti-correlated, but we can engineer the correlation we want — e.g. both qubits always agreeing (both 0 or both 1). Tie many qubits together this way and one measurement ripples knowledge across the whole set. Combine that with superposition's parallel possibilities and interference's steering, and you have the machinery behind every quantum speed-up.

Why it matters: superposition + interference + entanglement is the entire toolbox. From here on it's notation — a tidier way to write states, starting with bra-ket.
