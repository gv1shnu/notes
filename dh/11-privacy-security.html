Privacy & Security

Difficulty: Intermediate

Large digital archives often contain financial records, personal photographs, research, creative work, software licenses and other irreplaceable information. Protecting an archive is not only about preventing data loss—it is also about ensuring that only authorized people can access or modify it.

Confidentiality, Integrity & Availability

ObjectiveArchive Example
ConfidentialityOnly authorized users can read private files.
IntegrityFiles remain accurate and unmodified.
AvailabilityThe archive can be accessed when needed.

A well-managed archive balances all three objectives rather than focusing on only one.

Access Control

Not every archive should be accessible to every user or device.

   │
   ├── Personal
   ├── Shared

Separate collections according to who requires access. This simplifies collaboration while reducing accidental exposure.

Read-Only Archives

Completed collections that rarely change can benefit from read-only storage or write-protected media. Preventing accidental modification is often as valuable as preventing unauthorized access.

Encryption for Archives

Archives stored on portable drives or transported between locations may benefit from encryption. Encryption protects data if the storage device is lost or stolen.

Store recovery information separately and periodically confirm that encrypted archives can still be opened.

Sharing Archives

Before sharing an archive, review what it contains.

  • Remove temporary files.
  • Exclude unnecessary duplicates.
  • Check embedded metadata.
  • Verify licenses before redistributing software.
  • Share only the required subset of files.

Physical Protection

RiskMitigation
WaterStore backups in protective containers.
HeatAvoid prolonged exposure to high temperatures.
ShockHandle mechanical drives carefully.
TheftKeep portable archives secured when not in use.

Documentation

Large collections become difficult to understand over time. Maintain simple documentation describing folder structure, naming conventions, backup schedules, storage locations and restoration procedures.

Key Takeaway

An archive should remain private, trustworthy and accessible. Good archive security comes from sensible access control, careful sharing, physical protection and clear documentation rather than relying on a single technology.
