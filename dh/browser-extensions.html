Browser extensions

Difficulty: Easy

A command line is the right tool for a bulk job. For a single tweet or a Saved folder you want to prune on a Sunday, a button in the browser is faster than a terminal. Two that come up a lot:

Twitter Media Harvester

Adds a small download button under each tweet. Click it and you get the image, GIF, or video at full resolution — not the downscaled preview a right-click "save image" hands you — with a filename that actually says which tweet it came from. It's the "I just want this one" answer to gallery-dl's "I want the whole account." Free and open-source.

Unsaver for Instagram

Instagram lets you save posts endlessly and gives you no way to clean up but tapping each one, twice, forever. Unsaver-style extensions batch that: select many, unsave in one pass, reclaim a Saved tab that had become a hoard.

Heads up on pricing: some of these Instagram tools are freemium — the basic unsave is free, but bulk or unlimited actions can sit behind a paid upgrade. Check before you rely on it; I'm not endorsing the paid tier, just noting it exists.

The trade-off with extensions

NiceNot nice
Zero setup, one click, in-contextWants broad permissions (“read all your data on this site”)
Great for one-off grabsBreaks the week the site reshuffles its HTML
No terminal requiredClosed-source ones are a trust black box

Rule of thumb: prefer open-source extensions, read the permissions before you install, and for anything you'll do more than a handful of times, drop back to yt-dlp or gallery-dl — a CLI can't quietly change hands or start showing ads.
