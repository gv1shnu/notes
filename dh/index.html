Data Hoarding

Subject level: Beginner to Intermediate

The art of collecting, organizing, preserving and maintaining large digital archives. Not "keep everything" and not "delete everything" — how to decide what deserves long-term preservation, how to get it onto your disk in the first place, and how to keep it findable and intact for years.

Part 1 · Acquiring the hoard

The tools that pull media, files and whole collections down to disk. All free / open-source unless a page flags a paid tier.

  1. yt-dlp — video and audio, from a thousand sites
  2. gallery-dl — images and whole galleries
  3. Browser extensions — Twitter and Instagram helpers
  4. Download managers — terminal and GUI, every OS
  5. FitGirl repacks — the compression legend

Who is this for?

  • People with growing collections of photos, videos and documents.
  • PC enthusiasts and homelab users.
  • Collectors of games, books, music and films.
  • Researchers, students, and digital-preservation enthusiasts.
  • Anyone who wants a clean, searchable, reliable archive.

What you will learn

  • How to acquire media and files efficiently, on any OS.
  • How to organize large collections and identify what's worth keeping.
  • Deduplication, metadata, and integrity verification.
  • Compression and archival formats.
  • Storage hardware, backup strategies, and long-term preservation.
