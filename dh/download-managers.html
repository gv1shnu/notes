Download managers

Difficulty: Medium

yt-dlp and gallery-dl know specific sites. A download manager doesn't care what the file is — it cares that the download finishes. Its whole job is the unglamorous stuff: resume after your Wi-Fi blinks, split one file across many connections to go faster, and queue a hundred links so you can walk away.

A word on the famous paid one

On Windows, IDM (Internet Download Manager) is the name everyone knows — and it's paid (shareware, a one-off licence). It's fine software, but the free stack above (aria2 / FDM / JDownloader) covers the same ground — resume, acceleration, queues — without the cheque. This section deliberately stays on the free/open-source side; IDM is mentioned only so you know the free tools aren't a downgrade.

Rule of thumb: reach for a manager when the file is the problem (huge, flaky link, many at once), and for yt-dlp / gallery-dl when the site is the problem.
