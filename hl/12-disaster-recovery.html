Disaster Recovery

Difficulty: Intermediate

Failures are inevitable. Hard drives fail, operating systems become corrupted, services stop working and users make mistakes. Disaster recovery is the process of restoring your homelab to a working state with minimal data loss and downtime.

What Can Go Wrong?

FailureExample
HardwareDisk or power supply failure.
SoftwareBroken update or misconfiguration.
User ErrorAccidental deletion.
SecurityCompromised service.
EnvironmentFire, water or theft.

What Should Be Backed Up?

  • Configuration files.
  • Virtual machine images.
  • Container volumes.
  • Databases.
  • User documents and media.
  • Automation workflows.

Key Takeaway

Disaster recovery is more than backups. It includes planning, documentation, testing and a repeatable restoration process. A homelab that can be rebuilt quickly is far more valuable than one that merely stores backups.
