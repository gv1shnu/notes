Reverse Proxies

Difficulty: Intermediate

As your homelab grows, you may have dozens of services running on different ports. A reverse proxy provides a single entry point, forwarding incoming requests to the appropriate application while hiding the internal complexity.

Without a Reverse Proxy

192.168.1.10:3000
192.168.1.10:8080
192.168.1.10:8096
192.168.1.10:9443
192.168.1.10:2283

Remembering ports quickly becomes inconvenient as more applications are deployed.

How It Works

The application never communicates directly with the user. Every request first passes through the reverse proxy.

Benefits

  • One public entry point.
  • Readable hostnames instead of ports.
  • Centralized HTTPS configuration.
  • Simpler authentication integration.
  • Easier expansion as new services are added.

Key Takeaway

Reverse proxies simplify access to self-hosted applications by providing a single, organized gateway. They improve usability, centralize request handling and prepare the homelab for secure growth as additional services are deployed.
