Self-Hosting Services

Difficulty: Intermediate

Building a server is only the beginning. The real value of a homelab comes from the services it provides. Self-hosting means running applications on infrastructure that you control instead of relying entirely on third-party providers.

What Can You Self-Host?

Most modern self-hosted applications are distributed as containers, making deployment and updates straightforward.

Common Categories

CategoryPurpose

Media ServerStream music, movies and TV shows.
Password ManagerManage credentials securely.
Document ManagementArchive and search scanned documents.

Why Self-Host?

  • Centralize your personal services.
  • Customize applications to your workflow.
  • Reduce dependence on multiple providers.
  • Learn modern infrastructure technologies.
  • Create a foundation for future automation and AI.

Trade-offs

BenefitResponsibility
Greater control.Maintain updates.
Custom configuration.Troubleshoot issues.
Local ownership.Monitor uptime and backups.

Key Takeaway

Self-hosting transforms a server into a useful platform. Rather than installing applications directly on your personal computer, services are centralized, easier to maintain and accessible to multiple devices across your network.
