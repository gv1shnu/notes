Storage Planning

Difficulty: Beginner

Storage is the foundation of every homelab. Applications can be reinstalled, but data often cannot be replaced. Designing storage before deploying services makes future expansion much easier.

Think Beyond Capacity

When selecting storage, consider more than just terabytes.

FactorWhy It Matters
CapacityHow much data can be stored.
PerformanceApplication responsiveness.
ReliabilityLong-term operation.
ExpandabilityFuture growth.
CostTotal cost of ownership.

Separate Workloads

└── HDD

Keeping frequently accessed data on SSDs while storing bulk data on HDDs balances performance and cost.

Plan for Growth

A homelab often expands faster than expected. Leave unused drive bays, SATA ports or M.2 slots available if possible.

Keep Data Organized

Separate storage according to purpose rather than placing everything in a single directory. 

Storage/

├── Backups/
├── Media/
├── Projects/
├── VirtualMachines/
└── Shared/

Shared Storage

As your homelab grows, multiple devices may need access to the same files. A central storage location avoids maintaining separate copies on every computer.

      │

      │

Leave Free Space

Running a drive completely full reduces flexibility and can negatively affect performance. Maintain free capacity for updates, snapshots and temporary files.

Document Your Layout

Even a simple text file describing where important data is stored can save considerable time during upgrades or troubleshooting. 

README.md

OS  → SSD

Key Takeaway

Good storage planning focuses on organization and future growth as much as capacity. Design a structure that separates workloads, keeps important data centralized and can expand without major reorganization.
