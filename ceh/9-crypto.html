Cryptography

Difficulty: Hard

Cryptography rarely fails because someone broke the maths. It fails because of misuse — the right algorithm used the wrong way, or the wrong algorithm used at all. As a tester you are looking for those mistakes, not trying to crack AES.

What to look for

Mistake Why it hurts
Weak algorithms (MD5, SHA-1, DES)Broken or brute-forceable; unfit for passwords or signatures
Bad randomnessPredictable tokens, keys, and session IDs
Homegrown cryptoAlmost always weaker than the standard it replaced
Poor key storageKeys in code or config defeat the whole scheme
Missing cert validationTLS that does not check the certificate invites interception
Old TLS versionsDowngrade and known protocol weaknesses
Secrets committed to codeAPI keys and passwords in the repo history

Finding it

Most crypto findings surface from other phases. testssl.sh flags weak TLS and ciphers. Fingerprinting reveals cookie and token structure (a base64 blob that decodes cleanly is worth a look). Source or APK review turns up md5(password) and hardcoded keys: 

$ grep -rniE 'md5|sha1|DES|ECB' src/
src/auth.py:42:  pw_hash = hashlib.md5(password.encode()).hexdigest()

An MD5 password hash is a clean finding with a clean fix: use a slow, salted algorithm (bcrypt, scrypt, Argon2). The OWASP cryptographic storage cheat sheet is the reference for what "right" looks like.
