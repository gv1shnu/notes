Password attacks

Difficulty: Medium

Credentials are the most common way in, and testing them needs extra care because account lockouts and password sprays can hurt real users. Before any attempt, confirm test accounts, the allowed rate, the time window, and whether production accounts are off limits.

Online: guessing against a live service

hydra tries username/password pairs against a running service. Keep the thread count low and use a small, targeted list — not the whole of rockyou against a real account: 

$ hydra -l labuser -P small.txt ssh://10.10.10.5 -t 4 -f
[DATA] attacking ssh://10.10.10.5:22
[22][ssh] host: 10.10.10.5   login: labuser   password: Summer2024!
1 of 1 target successfully completed

Spraying, not hammering. To avoid lockouts, spray one password across many users with a long delay, rather than many passwords at one user. Do the timing maths against the lockout policy (say, 5 attempts per 30 minutes) and stay well under it. Locking out a company's staff is a self-inflicted denial of service.

Offline: cracking a captured hash

If enumeration or a foothold yields password hashes, cracking happens offline — no lockouts, no noise. First identify the hash, then throw a wordlist at it: 

$ hashid '$6$xyz$...'
[+] SHA-512 Crypt

$ hashcat -m 1800 hash.txt rockyou.txt      # -m 1800 = sha512crypt
$ john --wordlist=rockyou.txt hash.txt      # john picks the format itself
labuser:Summer2024!
Hash hashcat mode
MD5-m 0
NTLM (Windows)-m 1000
sha512crypt ($6$)-m 1800
bcrypt ($2b$)-m 3200

Never commit cracked passwords into a notes repo, and treat them as live secrets. A cracked service-account password is often the pivot into the next system — which is where the chaining page picks up.
