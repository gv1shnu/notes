Wireless

The platform chapters cover different surfaces — wireless, mobile, IoT, cloud, crypto — but the mental model is the same each time: identify assets, understand trust boundaries, find weak configuration, validate safely, and report the business risk. Wireless is where that model meets radio and physical proximity.

What to check

  • Encryption in use — open, WEP (broken), WPA2, WPA3
  • Weak or default pre-shared keys
  • WPS left enabled (a known shortcut past the passphrase)
  • Guest network isolation — can a guest reach the internal network?
  • Rogue and evil-twin access points impersonating the real SSID
  • Whether Wi-Fi bridges into sensitive internal segments

A quick survey with a tool like airodump-ng lists nearby SSIDs, their encryption, and connected clients: 

BSSID              PWR  ENC   CIPHER  AUTH  ESSID
AA:BB:CC:00:11:22  -42  WPA2  CCMP    PSK   CorpWiFi
AA:BB:CC:00:11:23  -44  OPN                 CorpGuest

The open CorpGuest is worth a careful look: the real question is not "can I join it" but "once joined, can I reach anything I shouldn't?" Wireless testing needs the right hardware and, often, physical presence — if you do not have a dedicated test radio, document the methodology and the limitation rather than improvising on production gear.
