Reporting

Difficulty: Easy

Reporting is where the pentest becomes useful. A finding is not "I ran a tool." A finding is a clear risk with evidence, affected assets, business impact, reproduction steps, and a fix. Everything in the earlier phases only matters if it lands here in a form someone can act on.

Report shape

  1. Executive summary (for a non-technical reader)
  2. Scope and rules of engagement
  3. Methodology and timeline
  4. Risk-rating method
  5. Findings, ordered by severity
  6. Evidence and reproduction notes
  7. Remediation guidance
  8. Appendix: raw scan data, tool versions, target inventory

The finding template

Field What to write
TitleShort and specific. "Public S3 bucket exposes invoices" beats "Cloud issue."
SeverityCritical / High / Medium / Low / Info, with reasoning, not vibes.
Affected assetsHosts, URLs, accounts, apps, regions, components.
EvidenceRequests, responses, command output, timestamps, the test account used.
ImpactWhat an attacker could do, and why the business should care.
FixConcrete remediation and a quick way to validate it.

A worked example

Title:      Unauthenticated SMB share exposes backup archive
Severity:   High
Affected:   10.10.10.45 (filesrv.lab.local), TCP/445
Evidence:   enum4linux-ng share listing; smbclient dir of //filesrv/backups
Impact:     Any network user can read backup.zip containing legacy app config
            with database credentials.
Reproduce:  1. enum4linux-ng -A 10.10.10.45
            2. smbclient //10.10.10.45/backups -N
            3. get backup.zip   (lab only; deleted after test)
Fix:        Disable guest/anonymous access; ACL shares to authenticated users;
            rotate the exposed credentials.
Retest:     Confirm the share requires valid domain auth; verify rotation ticket.

Evidence rules

  • Capture enough proof to be reproducible.
  • Redact secrets, personal data, tokens, and unnecessary customer data.
  • Keep timestamps and test-account names.
  • Do not paste giant raw dumps into the main report — use the appendix.
  • Separate facts from assumptions.

A screenshot without context is just a ransom note from your past self. Write the finding so a developer who was never in the room can reproduce and fix it.
