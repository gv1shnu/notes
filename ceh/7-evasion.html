IDS, firewall, and honeypot awareness

Difficulty: Hard

Courses often frame this as "how do I sneak past the defences?" In a professional test the better question is "which controls see this activity, and which do not?" Evasion is most useful rewritten as control validation — the output is a map of detection gaps the blue team can close.

The three things in your way

Control What it does
FirewallAllows or blocks traffic by rule. Explains "filtered" ports.
IDS / IPSWatches for known-bad patterns; IPS can also block them.
HoneypotA fake, attractive target that exists only to catch and study attackers.

Validate, don't just dodge

For a given scan or action, the valuable deliverable is which logs and alerts should have fired:

  • Windows: 4624 / 4625 (logon success/fail), 4688 (process created), Sysmon event 3 (network connection)
  • Linux: auth.log, auditd
  • Network: an IDS alert on a port sweep, a firewall deny log

Then compare with what actually fired. A quiet SOC during a noisy scan is itself a finding. And stay alert for honeypots — a host that is suspiciously open, with every port answering and nothing that behaves like a real service, may be there to watch you.

A last caution: network actions can affect more than the target host. Confirm broadcast domains, monitoring, rate limits, and out-of-scope systems before testing.
