Reconnaisance aka Footprinting is the first step of the process of pentesting, where we need to find as much relevant information as possible on the target organisation and network. Relevance in this context, is information should be related to personnel or infrastructure.

I would recommend against surveilling the personnel for finding their vulnerability. That ain't pentesting at all, and if you ain't a PI, it's outright creepy and criminal.

Passive Recon

Gathering information that can be collected without direct contact with the organisation or their network or their personnel. No direct contact here intends no detection. Sources:

  • Organisation website
  • Social media profiles
  • Paid Services

Open source intelligence (OSINT) is the process of collecting and analyzing information about the target from publicly available sources, one of the most common phase of Passive recon.

One of the common tools for this are the Search Engines.: Google, Microsoft Bing, Yahoo, Brave, Yandex, Duckduckgo, SearXNG - an open source metasearch engine which collects Search Engine Result Pages (SERPs) from various search engines, functional but not optimal.

What are we looking for in Passive Recon?

  • Owner information
  • Subdomains
  • IP addresses
  • Employee names and emails
  • Technologies used
  • Physical locations
  • Company news and events, and so on.

Domains and subdomains

Subdomains are the domains that are part of the main domain. For example, if the main domain is example.com, then mail.example.com and blog.example.com are subdomains.

Finding the IP address of the main domain and subdomains can be useful for finding the location of the servers and the technologies used.

Site Report

Third party free websites like Netcraft can be used to obtain site information such as Subdomains, Trackers, background information, technologies used, Operating system used by servers, etc.
