Box types

Difficulty: Easy

Hats describe intent. Boxes describe something different: how much the tester knows before they start. It is a knob on the engagement, set by the client, not a description of the person.

Box Starting knowledge Simulates
White-box Full internal knowledge: source code, architecture, credentials. A malicious insider, or a thorough audit.
Black-box Nothing but a name or a URL. An outside attacker with no help.
Grey-box Partial knowledge, such as a normal user account. A regular user who turns hostile, or a phished employee.

These notes lean towards the black-box view, because it is the hardest and most honest starting point: you begin with almost nothing, and everything you later do is built from what you discover yourself. An endpoint you never find is a vulnerability you never test, so the early phases carry more weight than they first appear to.

Keep the two ideas separate in your head. Hats are about intent and legality; boxes are about how much the tester knows going in. Different knobs, same machine.
