Canonical problems

7. Producer–consumer.

Producers add data to a queue; consumers pull or subscribe. This is how message queues work.

Producers → Queue → Consumers
When the queue overflows, the strategy depends on what the data is for:
If… Then
recent data matters most drop the oldest
a sample is enough drop at random
all data matters spill to slower storage, tell producers to slow down

Risk: if the producer index runs ahead of the consumer index, you get races and overwritten data.
