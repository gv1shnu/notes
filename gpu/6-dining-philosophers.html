Canonical problems

Difficulty: Medium

A few classic problems show up again and again. Solve the canonical form and you solve every look-alike.

6. Dining philosophers.

Philosophers sit around a table. Each needs two forks to eat, shares each fork with a neighbour, and can do only one thing at a time.

Naive plan: pick up left fork, then right, eat, put both down.
Everyone, at once Result
grab left fork holds 1 fork
reach for right fork taken by neighbour → all starve

Everyone grabs their left fork at the same instant (blue lines). Now all five forks are taken and every philosopher needs a second one held by a neighbour — a circular wait, so all five starve. The fix breaks the symmetry: make one philosopher reach right-first, or allow only four at the table at once.

It is just the earlier pitfalls in disguise:

Behaviour Pitfall
politely competing for a fork resource contention
never dropping a held fork deadlock
all drop and re-grab together livelock

Solution
Idea Effect
Central authority decides who eats and when
Fork order break the symmetry so no full circle of waiting

No fix is trivially easy — think before you write the code.
