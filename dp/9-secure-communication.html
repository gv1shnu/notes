Secure Communication

Difficulty: Medium

Every message travels through multiple systems before reaching its recipient. Privacy depends not only on who you are talking to, but also on who can observe, store or modify the communication along the way.

Secure communication aims to protect three properties:

  • Confidentiality — only intended recipients can read the message.
  • Integrity — the message cannot be modified without detection.
  • Authenticity — you know who sent the message.

Without encryption, anyone with access to the communication path may be able to inspect its contents. Modern messaging services therefore encrypt messages before transmission.

Protecting content does not automatically hide communication patterns.

Verification

Many secure messaging applications allow users to verify encryption keys or safety numbers. Verification helps ensure that you are communicating with the intended person and not an attacker performing a man-in-the-middle attack.

Good Practices

  • Keep applications updated.
  • Enable multi-factor authentication where available.
  • Verify contacts for highly sensitive conversations.
  • Be cautious when sharing files, screenshots and cloud backups.
  • Remember that recipients can always copy or photograph messages.

Key Takeaway

Secure communication protects message contents, but privacy also depends on metadata, device security and the people participating in the conversation.
