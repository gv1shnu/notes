Your Digital Footprint

Difficulty: Easy

A digital footprint is the collection of information generated whenever you interact with technology. Every website you visit, every application you install, every message you send, and every online purchase contributes another piece of information about you.

Many people assume their identity online is limited to their name or email address. In reality, your identity is built from hundreds of small attributes that, when combined, become highly identifying.

Your Digital Identity

Think of your identity as a collection of attributes rather than a single username. 

You
├── Name

Removing one attribute rarely makes you anonymous. Modern systems correlate multiple identifiers to reconnect seemingly unrelated activities.

Explicit vs Implicit Information

Not all information is collected in the same way.

Explicit information is data you intentionally provide.

  • Name
  • Email address
  • Phone number
  • Date of birth
  • Shipping address
  • Payment information

Implicit information is generated automatically while using technology.

  • Public IP address
  • Browser fingerprint
  • Screen resolution
  • Installed fonts
  • Operating system
  • Language
  • Time zone
  • Mouse movement patterns
  • Typing behaviour
  • Approximate location

Most tracking today relies more heavily on implicit information than explicit information.

Correlation

Individual websites may know very little about you. Large tracking companies combine information from many different sources to build a much more complete profile. 

Website A
    │
Cookie
    │
Tracking Network
    │

    │

This process is called correlation. Linking independent pieces of information is one of the primary techniques used for advertising, analytics, and identity resolution.

Key Takeaway

Privacy is rarely lost because of a single piece of information. Instead, multiple identifiers are linked together until they uniquely describe one individual.
