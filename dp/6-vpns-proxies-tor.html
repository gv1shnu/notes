VPNs, Proxies & Tor

Terms such as VPN, Proxy and Tor are often used interchangeably. They are not the same technology and they solve different problems. Choosing the wrong tool usually comes from misunderstanding your threat model.

Common Misconceptions

  • "A VPN makes me anonymous." — No. It changes who you trust; it does not eliminate trust.
  • "Incognito mode hides my IP." — No. Private browsing only affects local browser storage.
  • "Tor is only for criminals." — No. It is a legitimate anonymity network used by journalists, researchers, activists and ordinary users.
  • "HTTPS and VPNs are the same." — No. HTTPS encrypts traffic between your browser and a website. A VPN encrypts traffic between your device and the VPN server.

Which One Should You Use?

GoalRecommended Tool
Protect traffic on public Wi-FiVPN

Maximise anonymityTor
Access geo-restricted servicesVPN or Proxy (where permitted)

The correct choice depends entirely on your threat model discussed earlier. No single technology is universally superior.

Key Takeaway

Privacy tools change what different parties can observe, but they do not make you invisible. Understanding who can see your activity before and after using a tool is more important than memorising product names.
