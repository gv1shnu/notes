Identity & Compartmentalization

Difficulty: Medium

One of the biggest privacy mistakes is using the same identity everywhere. If every service knows your real name, primary email address, phone number, device and payment method, it becomes trivial to correlate your activities.

Compartmentalization is the practice of separating different parts of your digital life so that compromising one identity does not automatically expose the others.

Email Aliases

Your primary email address often becomes the key used to connect many online accounts. Reusing it everywhere increases the impact of a single breach.

Many Accounts Become Linked

Instead, consider separate addresses or email aliases for banking, shopping, personal communication and disposable registrations.

Browser Profiles

Modern browsers allow multiple profiles, each with its own cookies, history, extensions and saved logins. 

Browser

├── Personal
├── Work
├── Development
└── Research

This prevents unrelated websites from sharing the same browser state and reduces accidental cross-contamination between identities.

What Not to Mix

  • Personal and work accounts.
  • Anonymous forums and real-name social media.
  • Research activities and everyday browsing.
  • Disposable registrations and important accounts.

Trade-offs

BenefitCost
Reduced account linkageMore accounts to manage.
Smaller impact from breaches.Additional organisation.
Improved privacy.Less convenience.

Key Takeaway

Privacy is not only about hiding information. It is also about preventing different pieces of information from being linked together. Separating identities, email addresses, browser profiles and passwords limits how much can be learned if one account is compromised.
