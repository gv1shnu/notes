Before discussing privacy, anonymity and security, it helps to establish a common vocabulary. Throughout this course, these terms will appear repeatedly. Understanding them now will make the remaining chapters easier to follow.

Metadata

Metadata is information about data rather than the data itself.

Message ContentMetadata
"Meeting at 8 PM." Sender, recipient, time, IP address, device, message size.

Even when content is encrypted, metadata can still reveal communication patterns.

Cookies

Cookies are small pieces of data stored by your browser on behalf of a website. They allow websites to remember login sessions, preferences and shopping carts, but they may also be used for tracking.

Browser Fingerprinting

A browser fingerprint is created by combining information such as browser version, screen resolution, installed fonts, language and graphics capabilities to distinguish one browser from another.

Telemetry

Telemetry is diagnostic or usage information automatically sent from software to its developer. It may include crash reports, feature usage statistics, device information or performance metrics.

Threat Model

A threat model describes what you want to protect, who you want to protect it from, how capable the adversary is, and the consequences if they succeed.

Remember These Principles

  • Privacy controls information disclosure.
  • Security protects information.
  • Anonymity reduces identity linkage.
  • No single tool solves every privacy problem.
  • Your threat model determines the appropriate solution.

Before You Continue

You do not need to memorize every networking protocol or cryptographic algorithm to benefit from this course. Focus on understanding the relationships between systems, how information moves, and why different privacy techniques exist. The remaining chapters will build upon these foundations gradually.
