LLMs & Local Models

Difficulty: Beginner

Every AI assistant is powered by one or more Large Language Models (LLMs). These models understand natural language, reason about information and generate responses. Choosing where the model runs—locally or in the cloud—is one of the most important design decisions when building a personal AI assistant.

An LLM predicts the most appropriate sequence of tokens based on the information it has received. It does not inherently know your files, applications or personal history unless that information is provided through memory or external tools.

Running Models Locally

Modern consumer hardware is capable of running increasingly capable language models. Performance depends on available memory, processor architecture and GPU acceleration. The goal is not necessarily to match the largest cloud models, but to achieve useful, private and responsive assistance.

Key Takeaway

The language model is the reasoning engine of a personal AI assistant, but it is not the entire assistant. Memory, tools, automation and local data are equally important. The most capable systems combine these components into a single architecture tailored to the user's own computing environment.
